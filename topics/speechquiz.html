Direct and Indirect Speech Quiz

Instructions

Question 1

Convert to indirect speech: "I am reading a book," said John.

Correct answer: John said that he was reading a book.

When converting from direct to indirect speech, present tense ("am reading") changes to past tense ("was reading") and pronouns change from first person to third person ("I" becomes "he").

Question 2

Convert to direct speech: The teacher asked if we had completed our assignments.

Correct answer: The teacher said, "Have you completed your assignments?"

When converting from indirect to direct speech, a reported question introduced by "if" or "whether" becomes a direct question. The past perfect "had completed" changes to present perfect "have completed".

Question 3

Convert to indirect speech: She said, "I will visit Paris next summer."

Correct answer: She said she would visit Paris the following summer.

When converting to indirect speech, future tense ("will visit") changes to conditional ("would visit") and time expressions change ("next summer" becomes "the following summer").

Question 4

Convert to direct speech: He told me that he had lost his wallet yesterday.

Correct answer: He said, "I lost my wallet yesterday."

When converting to direct speech, past perfect tense ("had lost") often reverts to simple past ("lost") when there's a specific time reference.

Question 5

Convert to indirect speech: "Don't touch the wire," the electrician warned.

Correct answer: The electrician warned not to touch the wire.

When converting imperative sentences (commands) to indirect speech, we use "to" or "not to" + infinitive verb form.

Question 6

Which of the following correctly represents indirect speech?

Correct answer: She said that she could solve that problem.

This is correct indirect speech because it uses "said that" to report, changes modal "can" to "could", and changes "this" to "that" (demonstrative pronouns often change in indirect speech).

Question 7

Convert to indirect speech: "Where do you live?" asked the stranger.

Correct answer: The stranger asked where I lived.

When converting direct questions to indirect speech, the question word order changes to statement word order (no auxiliary "do/does" required) and present tense ("do live") changes to past tense ("lived").

Question 8

Convert to direct speech: The doctor advised the patient to take rest.

Correct answer: The doctor said, "Take rest."

When "advise" is used in indirect speech for giving instructions, it typically translates to an imperative sentence in direct speech.

Question 9

Convert to indirect speech: "I have finished my homework," she said.

Correct answer: She said she had finished her homework.

When converting to indirect speech, present perfect tense ("have finished") changes to past perfect tense ("had finished").

Question 10

Convert to direct speech: My friend asked me if I was going to the party that night.

Correct answer: My friend said, "Are you going to the party tonight?"

When converting from indirect to direct speech, past tense ("was going") changes back to present tense ("are going") and "that night" changes to "tonight" for the immediate time reference.

Question 11

Convert to indirect speech: "I saw her yesterday," he told me.

Correct answer: He told me that he had seen her the day before.

When converting to indirect speech, simple past tense ("saw") changes to past perfect ("had seen") and time references shift ("yesterday" becomes "the day before").

Question 12

Convert to direct speech: She asked me how long I had been living in Paris.

Correct answer: She said, "How long have you been living in Paris?"

When converting to direct speech, past perfect continuous tense ("had been living") changes to present perfect continuous ("have been living").

Question 13

Which is the correct indirect speech for: "Let's go to the park," suggested Tom.

Correct answer: Both A and B are correct.

For suggestions using "let's," both "suggested that we go" and "suggested going" are acceptable forms of indirect speech.

Question 14

Convert to indirect speech: "I'll call you tomorrow," she promised.

Correct answer: She promised she would call me the next day.

When converting to indirect speech, future tense ("will call") changes to conditional ("would call") and time expressions change ("tomorrow" becomes "the next day").

Question 15

Which sentence is in direct speech?

Correct answer: "This is the best movie I've ever seen!" exclaimed Mark.

Direct speech is indicated by quotation marks and reports the exact words spoken, followed by a reporting verb.

Question 16

Convert to indirect speech: "We have been waiting for an hour," they complained.

Correct answer: They complained that they had been waiting for an hour.

When converting to indirect speech, present perfect continuous ("have been waiting") changes to past perfect continuous ("had been waiting").

Question 17

Convert to direct speech: He said he would finish the project by Friday.

Correct answer: He said, "I will finish the project by Friday."

When converting from indirect to direct speech, conditional ("would finish") changes back to future tense ("will finish") and pronouns change from third person to first person ("he" becomes "I").

Question 18

Convert to indirect speech: "Are you coming to the meeting tomorrow?" asked my colleague.

Correct answer: My colleague asked if I was coming to the meeting the next day.

When converting yes/no questions to indirect speech, we use "if" or "whether", change present tense to past tense, and shift time expressions ("tomorrow" becomes "the next day").

Question 19

Convert to direct speech: The tour guide advised us not to wander off alone.

Correct answer: The tour guide said, "Don't wander off alone."

When "advise not to" is used in indirect speech, it typically converts to a negative imperative (command) in direct speech.

Question 20

Convert to indirect speech: "I was studying when you called," said Mary.

Correct answer: Mary said she had been studying when I called.

When converting to indirect speech, past continuous ("was studying") generally changes to past perfect continuous ("had been studying"). The second clause ("you called") changes pronouns but remains in simple past tense.

Question 21

Which sentence correctly uses direct speech?

Correct answer: "Why are you late for the meeting?" she asked.

Direct speech uses quotation marks to report the exact words spoken, with the exact question structure preserved.

Question 22

Convert to direct speech: He wondered if it would rain the next day.

Correct answer: He said, "I wonder if it will rain tomorrow."

When the reporting verb is "wondered", it often indicates a thought or speculation rather than a direct question. In direct speech, this becomes "I wonder" followed by the question content.

Question 23

Convert to indirect speech: "This museum was built in 1890," the guide explained.

Correct answer: The guide explained that the museum was built in 1890.

When converting to indirect speech, demonstratives like "this" often change to "the", and the past tense ("was built") can remain as past tense since it refers to a historical fact.

Question 24

Convert to direct speech: She asked me what my favorite book was.

Correct answer: She said, "What is your favorite book?"

When converting questions from indirect to direct speech, past tense verbs ("was") change back to present tense ("is"), and the word order changes to direct question form.

Question 25

Convert to indirect speech: "Could you please help me with this package?" the old lady asked.

Correct answer: The old lady asked if I could please help her with that package.

When converting polite requests to indirect speech, we use "if" or "whether" followed by the same modal verb, and demonstratives change ("this" becomes "that").

Question 26

Convert to direct speech: She ordered him to leave immediately.

Correct answer: She said, "Leave immediately!"

When the reporting verb is "ordered," it typically converts to an imperative (command) in direct speech, often with an exclamation mark to show the forceful tone.

Question 27

Convert to indirect speech: "I've never been to Japan, but I'd love to go someday," said Paul.

Correct answer: Paul said he had never been to Japan, but he would love to go someday.

When converting to indirect speech, present perfect ("have never been") changes to past perfect ("had never been"), and conditional forms ("would love") remain the same.

Question 28

Convert to direct speech: My mother reminded me to take my medicine.

Correct answer: Both A and B are acceptable.

The reporting verb "reminded" can be expressed in direct speech using either "remember to" or "don't forget to" - both convey the same meaning of a reminder.

Question 29

Convert to indirect speech: "Hurry up or we'll miss the train!" shouted my friend.

Correct answer: My friend shouted that we should hurry up or we would miss the train.

When converting imperatives with explanations to indirect speech, the command often becomes "should" + verb, and future tense ("will miss") changes to conditional ("would miss").

Question 30

Which statement about direct and indirect speech is correct?

Correct answer: When converting from direct to indirect speech, pronouns, time expressions, and usually tenses change.

This is the fundamental rule of converting direct to indirect speech - changes typically occur in pronouns (I → he/she), time expressions (today → that day), and tenses (present → past).
