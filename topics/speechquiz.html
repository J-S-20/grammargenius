Direct and Indirect Speech Quiz

Question 1

Convert to indirect speech: "I am reading a book," said John.

Correct answer: John said that he was reading a book.

When converting from direct to indirect speech, present tense ("am reading") changes to past tense ("was reading") and pronouns change from first person to third person ("I" becomes "he").

Question 2

Convert to direct speech: The teacher asked if we had completed our assignments.

Correct answer: The teacher said, "Have you completed your assignments?"

When converting from indirect to direct speech, a reported question introduced by "if" or "whether" becomes a direct question. The past perfect "had completed" changes to present perfect "have completed".

Question 3

Convert to indirect speech: She said, "I will visit Paris next summer."

Correct answer: She said she would visit Paris the following summer.

When converting to indirect speech, future tense ("will visit") changes to conditional ("would visit") and time expressions change ("next summer" becomes "the following summer").

Question 4

Convert to direct speech: He told me that he had lost his wallet yesterday.

Correct answer: He said, "I lost my wallet yesterday."

When converting to direct speech, past perfect tense ("had lost") often reverts to simple past ("lost") when there's a specific time reference.

Question 5

Convert to indirect speech: "Don't touch the wire," the electrician warned.

Correct answer: The electrician warned not to touch the wire.

When converting imperative sentences (commands) to indirect speech, we use "to" or "not to" + infinitive verb form.

Question 6

Which of the following correctly represents indirect speech?

Correct answer: She said that she could solve that problem.

This is correct indirect speech because it uses "said that" to report, changes modal "can" to "could", and changes "this" to "that" (demonstrative pronouns often change in indirect speech).

Question 7

Convert to indirect speech: "Where do you live?" asked the stranger.

Correct answer: The stranger asked where I lived.

When converting direct questions to indirect speech, the question word order changes to statement word order (no auxiliary "do/does" required) and present tense ("do live") changes to past tense ("lived").

Question 8

Convert to direct speech: The doctor advised the patient to take rest.

Correct answer: The doctor said, "Take rest."

When "advise" is used in indirect speech for giving instructions, it typically translates to an imperative sentence in direct speech.

Question 9

Convert to indirect speech: "I have finished my homework," she said.

Correct answer: She said she had finished her homework.

When converting to indirect speech, present perfect tense ("have finished") changes to past perfect tense ("had finished").

Question 10

Convert to direct speech: My friend asked me if I was going to the party that night.

Correct answer: My friend said, "Are you going to the party tonight?"

When converting from indirect to direct speech, past tense ("was going") changes back to present tense ("are going") and "that night" changes to "tonight" for the immediate time reference.

Question 11

Convert to indirect speech: "I saw her yesterday," he told me.

Correct answer: He told me that he had seen her the day before.

When converting to indirect speech, simple past tense ("saw") changes to past perfect ("had seen") and time references shift ("yesterday" becomes "the day before").

Question 12

Convert to direct speech: She asked me how long I had been living in Paris.

Correct answer: She said, "How long have you been living in Paris?"

When converting to direct speech, past perfect continuous tense ("had been living") changes to present perfect continuous ("have been living").

Question 13

Which is the correct indirect speech for: "Let's go to the park," suggested Tom.

Correct answer: Both A and B are correct.

For suggestions using "let's," both "suggested that we go" and "suggested going" are acceptable forms of indirect speech.

Question 14

Convert to indirect speech: "I'll call you tomorrow," she promised.

Correct answer: She promised she would call me the next day.

When converting to indirect speech, future tense ("will call") changes to conditional ("would call") and time expressions change ("tomorrow" becomes "the next day").

Question 15

Which sentence is in direct speech?

Correct answer: "This is the best movie I've ever seen!" exclaimed Mark.

Direct speech is indicated by quotation marks and reports the exact words spoken, followed by a reporting verb.
