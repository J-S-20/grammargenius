English Tenses Explained

In grammar, tense refers to the form a verb takes to indicate the time of an action or event. English has three primary tenses (past, present, future), each with four aspects (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous), resulting in a total of 12 tenses.

Present Tenses

Tense Form Use Example
Simple Present Base verb (e.g., I work) or base verb + -s/-es (e.g., He works) Habits, facts, scheduled events I drink coffee every morning.
Present Continuous am/is/are + present participle (-ing) Actions happening now, temporary situations I am eating lunch.
Present Perfect have/has + past participle Completed actions with present relevance, experiences I have finished my report.
Present Perfect Continuous have/has + been + present participle (-ing) Actions in progress recently, actions continuing from the past I've been working on this project all day.

Past Tenses

Tense Form Use Example
Simple Past Past tense form of the verb Completed actions at a specific time in the past I went to the store yesterday.
Past Continuous was/were + present participle (-ing) Actions in progress at a specific time in the past I was watching TV when the phone rang.
Past Perfect had + past participle Actions completed before another action in the past I had finished my work before I went to bed.
Past Perfect Continuous had + been + present participle (-ing) Actions in progress before another action in the past I had been working on the project for hours before the deadline.

Future Tenses

Tense Form Use Example
Simple Future will + base verb Predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises It will rain tomorrow.
Future Continuous will + be + present participle (-ing) Actions in progress at a specific time in the future I will be working on this project all next week.
Future Perfect will + have + past participle Actions completed before a specific time in the future I will have finished the report by Friday.
Future Perfect Continuous will + have + been + present participle (-ing) Actions in progress for a period before a specific time in the future By next year, I will have been working here for ten years.
