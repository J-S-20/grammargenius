Parts of Speech

Noun

A word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

Types

  • Common Noun: A general name (e.g., The dog barked loudly.)
  • Proper Noun: A specific name (e.g., I live in London.)
  • Collective Noun: A group name (e.g., The team won the game.)
  • Abstract Noun: An idea or concept (e.g., Love is a powerful emotion.)

Pronoun

A word that replaces a noun.

Types

  • Personal: Refers to a person/thing (e.g., She went to the store.)
  • Possessive: Shows ownership (e.g., That book is mine.)
  • Demonstrative: Points out specifics (e.g., This is my favorite song.)
  • Interrogative: Asks questions (e.g., Who is at the door?)
  • Reflexive: Refers back to the subject (e.g., He hurt himself.)

Verb

A word that expresses action or being.

Types

  • Action: Shows an action (e.g., She runs every morning.)
  • Linking: Connects subject to description (e.g., He is a doctor.)
  • Auxiliary: Helps other verbs (e.g., I have finished my homework.)

Adjective

A word that describes a noun/pronoun.

Examples

  • The red car is fast.
  • She is a talented musician.

Adverb

A word that modifies a verb, adjective, or adverb.

Examples

  • He ran quickly.
  • She is very tall.

Preposition

A word showing relationship between words.

Examples

  • The book is on the table.
  • She went to the store.

Conjunction

A word that connects words, phrases, or clauses.

Types

  • Coordinating: Connects equal parts (e.g., I like apples and oranges.)
  • Subordinating: Connects dependent clause (e.g., He studied hard because he wanted to pass.)

Interjection

A word expressing strong emotion.

Examples

  • Ouch! That hurts.
  • Wow! That's amazing.
